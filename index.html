Think about this...

Every year the NSA recruits top talent from colleges and universities across the country for internships and full time positions. They promise students the thrill of serving their country, being a part of something bigger than themselves, and the chance to work with the latest technology.

Given recent leaks, the reality is much darker. Unfortunately, many students don't give enough thought to the nature of the NSA's work and the kind of activities they may either be directly, or indirectly, supporting. So if you're thinking about taking a position with the NSA, please consider the following.

  • Not Patriotic

    The NSA promises candidates that employment with the agency is a great opportunity to be patriotic, but a true patriot would protect both the Constitution and innocent people from an overbearing fedral government.

  • No Protection

    Despite what the President has said, if an employee of the NSA finds something morally reprehensible or believes agency activities to be unconstitutional, there's very little they can do to change that without suffering severe prosecution from the Federal government.

  • Compromising Your Integrity

    Currently, in order to work for the NSA, you must be able to put aside any doubts you may have about the agency's policies. For people with strong moral standings about liberty and freedom, the choice is simple: compromise your values or don't work for the government.

  • Culture of Spying & Lying

    Recently the NSA has implemented a "two-man" policy which encourages employees to spy on one another and report coworkers if there's any suspicion of they might be leaking information.


If you're not able to justify working for an organization that can't even follow the unconstitutional policies it setup for itself, is actively commandeering the Internet, pitting employees against each other, and aggressively going after anyone who tries to stop it, don't worry. You're not alone.

We're asking students like you to report any NSA recruiting events at your school by filling out this form. As we process submissions, we'll put them on our events page where the public can take action and express their concerns to each school's officials.
